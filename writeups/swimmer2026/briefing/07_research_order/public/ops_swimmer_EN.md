## **Investigation Structure in Operation SWIMMER**

## The connection between the future and the past is extremely unstable, and we can only secure a limited bandwidth for communication.  Therefore, we are seeking talented “**Collaborators**” who can research a large amount of information within a short time.

## We have asked **DIVER OSINT CTF**, which is active in 2026, to help us find such collaborators through a competitive CTF format.  We will handle events in the future, while the DIVER OSINT CTF team will support the systems operating in your time.

## **Investigation Targets**

Now to the main subject. We are currently tracking the following three individuals.  
In our time, obtaining information has become difficult; however, in 2026, it is likely that information about these three people can still be obtained.

### Target 1: rain

A person calling themselves “rain” has been confirmed to operate as a task force that creates and spreads false information. As of 2026, we have found no detailed clues other than that they describe themselves as a “traveler” (旅人) on social media.

### Target 2: debeyohiru

There is a person calling themselves “debeyohiru.” They appear to have a background in software engineering and are believed to be involved in tool development.

### Target 3: lilica

It has been discovered that a person calling themselves “lilica” is involved with the organization. They are believed to be responsible for design and recruiting within the organization. Around 2026, they were known to call themselves “Twilight Blossom Lilica (黄昏ブロッサムリリカ)”.

### The Final Mission

It has been discovered that the organization is composed of people with diverse backgrounds;  individuals who would be unlikely to meet in real life. Identifying the trigger that brought them together may help reveal what is truly happening.

### Social Situation Around 2025–2026 (research\_2025)

In our time, we are gradually losing access to accurate information from the past.  
To correct the accuracy of the information we currently have, please also research the real-world situation around 2025–2026.

## **Order of Investigation**

There are many investigation items (challenges), and they are not in any particular order. Please investigate and submit information as you discover it.  
However, some information may not be obtainable until other items are investigated first. For example, if you need to identify a person’s real name, you may need to locate their social media accounts before that.

## **Responsibilities of “Collaborators”**

What we ask of “Collaborators” is investigation. Not negotiation, contact, or the use of force.  
In other words, you must not interfere with or contact the investigation targets, attempt unauthorized access, attempt login actions, or report the targets via social media, etc.  
If any activity beyond this scope is confirmed, the operation will be deemed a failure.

If you are unsure like “This action seems necessary, but does it count as ‘contact’?” please ask the DIVER OSINT CTF team in the **\#ask-for-admin** channel.

## **Recording the Investigation Process**

Due to communication restrictions, you will be asked to submit only the results of your research.  
However, please keep as many notes as possible about your research process (e.g., hypotheses and evidence used to reach an answer). This will also be useful for sharing information within your team.  
In addition, you may publish your investigation records as a “writeup” after **03:10 p.m. on January 18, 2026 (UTC)** \-- in other words, after the mission is completed.  
Some challenges may have special rules regarding writeups, so please check them individually.

## **About “Hints”**

Even in our time, we want to continue investigating as much as possible.  
If we discover anything within the next 12 hours, we will submit it as a “hint” via the SWIMMER OSINT CTF Discord server.  
For each investigation item (challenge), we specify the time when hints can be released in advance.  
If teams have the same score, the team that answered first will be ranked higher. Therefore, if you aim for a higher rank, it is better to submit information without waiting for hints.

## **Handling OSINT Skills**

After completing this research, you will gain know-how regarding OSINT—intelligence using publicly available information.  
However, please understand that such know-how can be dangerous depending on how it is used. “Publicly available” does not mean “everything may be exposed.”  
OSINT also has limitations. It is dangerous to assume that “OSINT will give you all the information you need” or that “information obtained through OSINT is guaranteed to be accurate.”  
It is also not advisable to become overconfident and believe you “completely understand OSINT” after this research.  
We do not want our collaborators to misuse OSINT skills. Please use them for good, continue refining your skills, and always keep the risks and limitations in mind.
